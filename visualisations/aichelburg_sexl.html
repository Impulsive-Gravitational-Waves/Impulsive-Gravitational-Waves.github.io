Aichelburg-Sexl Solution

Aichelburg-Sexl solution is one of the simplest solutions representing exact impulsive gravitational waves. It is constructed as a ultra-relativistic boost of Schwarzchild spacetime, hence it can be considered as a field generated by massless point-like particle traveling at a speed of light. This solution has the form $$\mathrm{d} s^2 = -2 \mathrm{d}u \mathrm{d} v + 2 \mathrm{d} \eta \mathrm{d} \bar{\eta} + b \log (\eta \bar{\eta}) \delta (u) \mathrm{d} u^2. $$ It is the simplest solution in the family of impulsive waves generated by multipole structure. Coordinates $x, y$ are defined by $$\eta = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left(x + \mathrm{i} y\right)$$ and its complex conjugate $\bar{\eta}$.
