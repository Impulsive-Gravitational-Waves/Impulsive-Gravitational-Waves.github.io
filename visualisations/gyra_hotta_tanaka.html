Gyratonic Hotta-Tanaka Solution

Gyratonic metric with off-diagonal terms can be generalized to the case of non-zero cosmological constant. The metric in conformally flat coordinates then reads $$ \mathrm{d}s^2=\frac{2\mathrm{d}\eta~\mathrm{d}\bar{\eta} - 2 \mathrm{d}\mathcal{U}~\mathrm{d}\mathcal{V} + 2H(\eta, \bar{\eta}) \delta(\mathcal{U}) ~\mathrm{d}\mathcal{U}^2}{\left[1+\frac{1}{6}\Lambda(\eta \bar{\eta}-\mathcal{U}\mathcal{V})\right]^2} + \frac{2J\left(\eta, \bar{\eta}, \mathcal{U}\right) \mathrm{d}\eta~\mathrm{d}\mathcal{U} +2\overline{J}\left(\eta, \bar{\eta}, \mathcal{U}\right) \mathrm{d}\bar{\eta}~\mathrm{d}\mathcal{U}}{\left[1+\frac{1}{6}\Lambda(\eta \bar{\eta}-\mathcal{U}\mathcal{V})\right]^2}.$$ We will consider class of solutions analogous to the gyratonic Aichelburg-Sexl solution (in sense of generalizing it to the $\Lambda \neq 0$ case) and Hotta-Tanaka solution (in sense that the function $H$ is exactly the same). Thus, we have $J = \frac{\chi}{2 \mathrm{i} \eta} \Theta(\mathcal{u})$ for $\chi = const.$ and profile of Hotta-Tanaka solution.
