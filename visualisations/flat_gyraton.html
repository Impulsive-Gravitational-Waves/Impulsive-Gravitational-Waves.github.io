Gyratonic impulsive waves

One plausible generalisation of distributional metric $$ \mathrm{d}s^2 = - 2\mathrm{d} u \mathrm{d}v + 2 \mathrm{d}\eta \mathrm{d}\bar{\eta} + 2 H(\eta, \bar{\eta}) \delta(u) \mathrm{d}u^2 $$ is to add non-diagonal terms. Usually such terms are not considered as we can preform local coordinate transformation so non-diagonal terms vanish, however, such transformation changes the overall topology of spacetime. Off diagonal terms then carry information about rotatinal character of matter contained in such spacetime. Rotational sources were studied by Bonnor and corresponding spacetimes were independently rediscovered by Frolov and Fursaev. Here, we are going consider special case of following metric, $$ \mathrm{d}s^2 = - 2\mathrm{d} u \mathrm{d}v + 2 \mathrm{d}\eta \mathrm{d}\bar{\eta} + 2 H(\eta, \bar{\eta}) \delta(u) \mathrm{d}u^2 + 2 J(\eta, \bar{\eta}, u) \mathrm{d} u \mathrm{d} \eta + 2 \bar{J}(\eta, \bar{\eta}, u) \mathrm{d} u \mathrm{d} \bar{\eta}, $$ where we set $J = \frac{\chi}{2 \mathrm{i} \eta} \Theta(u)$ for $\chi = const.$ and consider the function $H$ to be in Aichelbug-Sexl form $H = b \log(2 \eta \bar{\eta})$. This results to a distributional form of a metric from Frolov-Fursaev gyraton family.
