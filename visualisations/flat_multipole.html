Multipole Sources Generating Impulsive Gravitational Waves (Flat Case)

Griffiths and Podolský described class of impulsive gravitational pp-waves generated by null particles with multipole structure. This solution is based on multipole expansion of separated solution to the two-dimensional wavefront Poisson equation in presence of point-like sources.

The general metric for impulsive pp-waves propagating in flat background is $$ \mathrm{d} s^2 = -2 \mathrm{d} u \mathrm{d} v + 2 \mathrm{d} \eta \mathrm{d} \bar{\eta} + 2 H(\eta, \bar{\eta}) \delta(u) \mathrm{d} u^2. $$ Field equations are reduced to two-dimensional Poisson equation on the wavefront $$ \Delta H = 4 H_{,\eta \bar{\eta}} = 8 \pi J(\eta, \bar{\eta}). $$ Epmloying polar coordinates $(\rho, \phi)$, we can consider solutions separated in form $H = \sum_{m=0}^\infty h(\rho) \exp(- i m \phi)$. Such solutions have the form $$ H = -b_0 \log \rho + \sum_{m=1}^\infty b_m \rho^{-m} \cos \left[m \left(\phi - \phi_m\right)\right], $$ where $b_m$ and $\phi_m$ are the parameters of multipole structure.

The simplest case is the Aichelburg-Sexl solution, where $b_m = 0$ for all $m \geq 1$.
